Welcome. This is zero-plus-plus. An extension to zeronet to accelerate the transition to decentralization and blockchain networks. Decentralization is a revolutionary way of thinking where there exists no single point of failure. Typically, stuff on the internet is stored in silos. There is a single server or a host serving the website. An attack on that server will lead to downtime and an unavailable service causing you a mild nusiance or stealing your identity. Decentralisation solves this by relying on multiple nodes instead of a central authority. These nodes form a consensus and serve agreed upon data. It is possible to change consensus by a 51% attack. This is where you control the majority of the nodes and rewrite the rules of the network. This is highly difficult though. Imagine you have a million unique nodes in your network. That would mean you need one more than half a million to successfully corrupt the data. Let's face it, that is a lot of devices. And that is only step 1. You have to break the encryption too. Then you have to ensure that your five hundred thousand and one devices (Of course assuming that your devices are among the initial million nodes, if not the number changes because it is 51% of total nodes) before the remaining nodes. See, not that easy to corrupt a decentralized network.

Blockchain technologies rely heavily (among other technologies) on the concept of asymmetric encryption. The core axiom of asymmetricity is that one side is unlike the other, which in computer science translates to one way computation. The simplest one way function is multiplication. It is easy to see that 29*31=899. It is relatively difficult to figure out that 899 is formed by multplying 29 and 31. There more complex one way hash functions (such as, SHA and RSA) used in cryptography. Indeed, as humanity progresses, so do cryptographic functions (Read more about the history of code making and code breaking in this wonderful book by Simon Singh). Currently, we use Elliptic Curve Cryptography in our most advanced blockchain projects.
